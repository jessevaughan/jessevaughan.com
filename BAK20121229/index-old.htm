I AM CONSTANTLY TAKING PHOTOGRAPHS.

I received my first 35mm camera as a Christmas present at the age of 14. It was a Canon AE-1 Program that, at the time, I had no idea how to use. My parents encouraged me to take a weekend photography class and from then on I proceeded to carry that Canon with me for the next four years of high school. Even though I was never formally a part of my high school photography program, I never stopped taking photos and developing film. When the time came to finally declare a major at UMBC, I sat down with a course book and debated between economics and the visual arts. It's been a long road since the Christmas of 2000, but I've enjoyed every minute and every photograph along the way.

Over the years I have worked with several different cameras of various formats ranging from medium format 120 and 4x5 film to Polaroids to consumer and professional level DSLRs. With the advent of the iPhone and it's extremely powerful on-board camera, I find it much easier to take quick snaps of everyday life which currently satisfies my need to constantly photograph the world around me.

The photos featured here are just a small sampling of some of my recent large projects as well as a few candid shots. Click anywhere on each photograph to bring up a brief description as well as a little background on each piece. To see some of my daily captures as well as a few older bodies of work, please visit my flickr page.

I AM A PRETTY NICE GUY.

Before moving into the graphic arts, I studied and practiced fine art photography at UMBC. I have always had a passion for photographic expression and constantly seek out Cartier-Bresson's 'decisive moment.' These are a few samples of my work ranging from digital to medium format film to polaroid transfers.

Since our mobile phones now surpass the quality of the DSLRs I was using as a freshman in college, I'm constantly snapping candid shots throughout my week and uploading them to my flickr or instagram.

Although my professional photography has been put on the back burner while I pursue my design and development career, I still break my camera out and photograph small events or candid parties. If you would like to see more of my photographic work, check out my flickr.
